Port to HTML5

30 May 2012

I have completed a port of the entire site from XHTML4 to HTML5. An important problem I had with XHTML4 was the additional xmlns=”http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml” attribute it was adding to elements of every template, page and field of the site. Thankfully, HTML5 is freeing me from all that.

However, HTML5 has caused some issues at the beginning. It didn't like my single empty elements, such as <div class="blah"/> or <span id="dah"/>. Yes, I miss the XML niceties of XHTML4. "Don't worry, XHTML5 is on its way!" I hear you saying. But, HTML5 has its own advantages too. A <br> without a slash is so comfortable. I have seen enough of HTML5 to adapt and stick to it for the foreseeable future. That is, until HTML6 comes along.
